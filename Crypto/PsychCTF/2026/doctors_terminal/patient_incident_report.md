Patient #4471 — Incident Report, Ward C

*Transcribed from a crumpled note found under Mattress 7, Bunk 3.*

I saw it. I actually saw it.

Last Tuesday during the 2am check-in, Dr. Mehta left his terminal unlocked for thirty-seven seconds while he went to refill his coffee. I counted. I always count.

He thinks we're too *stupid* to understand what he's doing. He thinks we can't read. He uses that website — the one with all the little puzzle pieces you drag and drop. I've watched him through the window reflection for weeks. He encrypts the patient files before he emails them. *Our* files. *My* file.

I managed to get a photo on the contraband phone we keep taped behind the emergency exit sign. The screen was glitching — water damage from the pipes incident — so the image came out corrupted. Part of the output is missing. The input (the actual file contents) is blacked out in my photo too, he had something covering it.

But the recipe is there. All four steps, clear as day.

And part of the ciphertext survived.

I need to know what he wrote about me.
